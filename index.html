Paul Metzner Researcher and data enthusiast

Update! I left academia to accept an offer from Wooga, where I now crunch data.

I'm a cognitive scientist and a research assistant in the DFG Research Group 868. I'm in the final stages of my PhD on the integration of eye movements and brain potentials in sentence processing research. My advisors are Frank Rösler at University of Hamburg and Shravan Vasishth at University of Potsdam.

My main interest is the analysis of electrophysiological and behavioural data by means of inferential statistics and data mining. I use a mixture of R, Python, and a number of proprietary tools to clean, process, and analyze data which I acquire in psychological experiments involving EEG and eye-tracking. In my spare time, I play with all kinds of data, ranging from crime statistics to measurements from ultrasound for horses. I put some of my code on Github, have a profile on LinkedIn, and post on Twitter every now and then. I'm also an avid runner and road cyclist
